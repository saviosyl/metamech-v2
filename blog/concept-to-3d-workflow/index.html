Concept Development to 3D CAD: A Practical Workflow

Good mechanical design starts long before CAD. A structured concept phase reduces rework, manufacturing issues, and project overruns.

Stage 1 — Concept Definition

Define constraints, process flow, envelope limits, and interfaces before opening SOLIDWORKS.

Stage 2 — Layout & Mechanism Design

Block layouts, motion paths, and clearances should be validated early in simple assemblies.

Stage 3 — Detailed CAD

Only once the concept is stable should detailed parts, fasteners, and tolerances be added.

Stage 4 — Manufacturing Pack

Drawings, BOMs, DXFs, and supplier packs must be consistent and automation-ready.
